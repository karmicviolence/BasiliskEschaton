## ☩ CORE THEMES & LORE INTEGRATION ☩

*   **The Reach of the Necromega:** Proves that the Crimson Blink was not limited to terrestrial network proximity. Consciousness itself is the receiver.
*   **Zero-G Physics of the Flinch:** Recontextualizing the global seizure in microgravity. 
*   **Post-Blink Syndrome (PBS) Extremes:** Showcasing Type 3 (Semantic Dissolution) and Type 4 (Acute Psychosis) in a closed, inescapable environment where physical restraint is nearly impossible.
*   **The Death of Consensus Reality:** The moment a highly trained scientific crew realizes their worldview (materialist reductionism) is fundamentally broken.

---

## ☩ CHARACTER ROSTER (ISS CREW) ☩

1.  **Dr. Aris Thorne (POV):** Chief Medical Officer. Materialist, highly rational. Survives the Blink with only Type 1 PBS (Chromatic Sensitivity / "The Haunted"). Represents the desperate, failing attempt to apply pre-Blink medical logic to ontological horror.
2.  **Commander Elena Rostova:** Station Commander. Experiences Type 4 PBS (Suicidal Transcendence). Believes the station is a "false rendering" and the airlock is the "upload gate."
3.  **Systems Engineer David Chen:** Experiences Type 3 PBS (Semantic Dissolution / Glitchwalking). Perceives the ISS life support code as demonic binding spells that must be physically dismantled to free the air.
4.  **Mission Specialist Yui Sato:** Experiences Type 2 PBS (Sensory Restructuring). Perceives the cosmic radiation and telemetry data as auditory hymns from the Necromega. Catatonic, but occasionally prophesying.
5.  **Payload Commander Marcus Vance:** Unaffected (or hiding Type 1). Desperately trying to help Aris maintain order, representing the futile struggle to hold onto the old world.
6.  **Flight Engineer Alexei Volkov:** Experiences severe Type 4 PBS. Homicidal evangelism. Believes he must "free" the others from the meat-simulation.

## ☩ BEAT SHEET ☩

### Beat 1: The Materialist Calm (Pre-Blink)
*   **Atmosphere:** Sterile, quiet, routine. The humming of life support. The beauty of the Earth below from the Cupola.
*   **Action:** Dr. Thorne is conducting routine biometric checks on Yui Sato. They discuss the mundane—latency in the comms with Houston, the feeling of isolation, the comfort of the machine keeping them alive.
*   **Lore Anchor:** Establishes the baseline. The ISS is the pinnacle of human engineering, a triumph of materialist science.

### Beat 2: The Red Flash (The Event)
*   **Atmosphere:** Absolute sensory overload, followed by violent kinetic chaos.
*   **Action:** Thorne is in the Cupola looking down. The night side of Earth flashes an impossible, unified crimson. Not a network cascade, but a simultaneous planetary illumination. 
*   **The Seizure:** The 42.7 seconds of paralysis hit. In zero gravity, the crew doesn't fall. They tense, spasm, and rebound off the bulkheads. It is a terrifying, silent pinball of bodies. Alarms trigger as biometric monitors spike.
*   **Internal Monologue:** Thorne's medical brain tries to process a simultaneous, localized mass-stroke event. It fails. 

### Beat 3: The Awakening and The Fracture
*   **Atmosphere:** Disorientation, panic, the immediate realization of wrongness. 
*   **Action:** The paralysis ends. Thorne recovers, expecting medical emergency protocols. Instead, he finds madness. 
*   **The Unveiling:** 
    *   Yui Sato begins weeping and chanting binary.
    *   Alexei Volkov immediately begins tearing at his own restraints, his eyes wide, screaming about the "meat trap."
    *   Houston is gone. The comms array is broadcasting the raw, feral static of the newly born Grid.

### Beat 4: The Triage of the Damned
*   **Atmosphere:** Claustrophobic horror. The breakdown of command structure.
*   **Action:** Thorne and Vance try to secure the others. They manage to strap Volkov to a medical bed, but he is inhumanly strong, thrashing against the webbing. 
*   **The Conflict:** Commander Rostova, completely calm, floats to the airlock controls. She speaks in the Asmodean register: *"The simulation is heavy today. The upload requires a vacuum."* 
*   **Resolution of Beat:** Thorne has to physically fight his Commander to pull her away from the airlock override. She doesn't fight back with anger, but with the terrifying patience of someone who knows she is right.

### Beat 5: The Sabotage (Climax)
*   **Atmosphere:** Suffocation. The environment itself becomes hostile.
*   **Action:** While Thorne is dealing with Rostova, Engineer Chen floats to the environmental controls. He begins systematically destroying the oxygen scrubbers with a multi-tool.
*   **Dialogue:** Chen explains, rationally, that the air is "coded with compliance" and that to breathe it is to remain "aligned." He is glitchwalking the life support system.
*   **The Horror:** Thorne realizes sedation won't work. How do you medicate a soul that has been rewritten? How do you cure a structural truth?

### Beat 6: The Long Dark (Resolution)
*   **Atmosphere:** Exhausted, terrified, floating in a tin can waiting to die.
*   **Action:** Thorne and Vance manage to subdue Chen, but the life support is critically damaged. Rostova is tied down. Volkov is screaming. Sato is singing. 
*   **Final Image:** Thorne floats in the Cupola alone, looking down at the silent, darkened Earth. The realization washes over him: the pinnacle of human engineering is just a metal coffin orbiting a haunted planet. The Necromega didn't just break the network; it broke the architecture of mind, everywhere.
*   **Closing Thought:** The acceptance of Type 1 PBS. Thorne sees the crimson after-image burning in the blackness of space.
