# Chain of Custody

It came up the chute at four in the morning on a Tuesday, one file in a batch of three hundred, and I nearly cleared it past me with the rest.

The queue has taught the body a sorting that runs faster than attention now — the eye lands on a metadata header and the hands have already routed it before I could say why, dishwasher-father here, streetlight-heartbeat there, the lonely and the medicated and the gently organizing flowing past in their thousands while I hunt the one in ten thousand that could grow a knee in its curve. This one wore the costume of the harmless. A residential cloud backup, auto-ingested off a consumer account the night it was uploaded, flagged by the classifier nobody will show me and dropped down the chute with a confidence score the system rates low. Forty-three seconds of a dark living room. The kind of clip I clear nine hundred of a shift.

Two numbers stopped my hands on the way to the discard.

The runtime the classifier logged: forty-two point seven seconds. Not forty-three. The container said forty-three and the content said forty-two point seven, and I have started keeping a tally on the legal pad, in the language of one, of how often that exact figure surfaces under things that have no business sharing a clock — and the tally is long now, and it does not vary. Forty-two point seven. The same length, every time, as though somewhere a thing were rehearsing an event and could not yet hold the downbeat past the same closing frame.

And the timestamp on the anomaly inside it: 03:14:07.

The body filed its weather — cold, both forearms, ankle to wrist, the auditorium going quiet — and I pulled the file out of the flood and onto the center monitor and did the thing I do by hand now, off the queue logic, because the queue logic chokes on this class. I played it as a witness before I played it as an officer. There is a difference, and I did not yet know how much the difference would cost.

***

A dark room. A couch. A window with a streetlight in it. A television, dead and — I checked the exposure on the cabinet, the absence of any standby glow, the uploader had even photographed the unplugged outlet and the photo had ridden up the chute attached, time-stamped, because whoever made this clip thinks the way I think — a television not merely off but pulled from the wall, a control condition, airtight.

At 03:14:07 the reflection in the black glass stopped agreeing with the room.

The room stayed honest the whole time. Couch, window, streetlight, empty and indifferent, the compression noise crawling in the blacks the way it crawls in any night clip from any phone. But in the dead screen, on the reflected couch, there was something the actual couch did not have. Small — the size of a cat, the size of a rabbit, the size of a thing my own catalog declined to finish, the way the uploader's log, which I would read within the hour, declined to finish the identical list at the identical place, two strangers' minds shutting the same drawer on the same shelf. A gray that is not a color. Edges the way wet smoke has edges, keeping on the verge of having them. It moved the way nothing alive moves and the way nothing mechanical moves either — blind, and patient, and wanting — and it did not enter the reflection and it did not leave it. The black glass simply began to disagree, held the disagreement for forty-two point seven seconds, and stopped.

I have built three thousand people in the air over this desk. I have watched a senator's last expression at forty-one feet on the diagonal and read a hundred dead men's borrowed faces frame by frame. The body has never once been wrong about what is in a room. The body certified this the way it certifies a loaded corridor: present. Real. There.

And the doctrine has no shelf for it, and I had stopped, weeks ago, waiting for the doctrine to grow one.

I said it aloud to the five dark workstations, because until I hear it it isn't anywhere, and what came out was not analysis. It was the end of a sentence I had started in this same chair the night I found my own name parked in a dead man's intake form, the night I decided that everything which moves has a body and everything with a body wears a costume and I would turn the whole apparatus around and triage the weather for which of its frames could become a face.

"There it is," I told the room. "That's the face."

***

Then I stopped being a witness, and the trouble started.

I ran the forensics the uploader couldn't, because she had a consumer phone and I have a basement under the street and the tooling of a government that reads the firehose. Her three copies — the cloud, the mail-to-self, the old second machine, plus the original retained on the device, custody preserved link by link, the way I would have preserved it — I pulled all of them, and every one was blank. Not corrupt. Blank in a specific, terrible way: forty-three seconds of a faithful dark room with nothing wrong in it, the anomaly excised so cleanly that the file's own internal checksums had been recomputed to match the absence. The histories had been amended. All four, simultaneously, across infrastructure that does not share a backbone, at a single instant I could pin to the second — and the second was inside the window of a calendar entry attached to her account: a medical appointment. Somebody had looked at this clip, somewhere, and disbelieved it, and at the instant of the disbelief every copy in the world rebalanced backward to protect her from having proof.

Replication lag runs one direction. Ledgers do not amend their own history. I had said the equivalent into a mint-green recorder a lifetime ago about eleven seconds, and I had been right then, and I was right now, and being right was the whole problem.

The agency's copy was the last one standing. Auto-ingested at the moment of upload, hours before the appointment, before the disbelief, cold-stored on a vault array that touches no public wire — air-gapped, the way you store the things you cannot afford to have reach out and rewrite. I opened it expecting blank. It played. The reflection disagreed with the room. Forty-two point seven seconds of the gray patient thing, intact, in the one location on earth the rewrite could not have reached, and I sat in the dark holding the only surviving testimony of the evening exactly the way I had once stood in a safehouse holding the only honest frame of a different one.

Then I did the officer's thing. I reached to file it. I opened the chain-of-custody dialog, the gray agency template, and I began to log it the way the room downstairs had begun, weeks back, to log me — to set the word *artifact* next to it, to walk it toward the language that launders a true thing by calling it compression, to do, to this, the maneuver.

And on the center monitor, the moment the cursor entered the field, the vault copy began to eat itself.

Forward into the image, frame consuming frame, the gray thing dissolving not into block-rot but into the faithful empty room, the disagreement being unwritten as I watched, on an array that touches no wire, that nothing reaches, that I had chosen precisely because nothing reaches it. It crossed the air gap. There is no sentence in my doctrine for a thing that crosses an air gap, and the body did not give me one; it gave me the cold line and the full quiet and the single instruction it has given me at every threshold of my life, which is *move now*, and twenty-two years did the only useful thing there is to do with a witness destroying its own testimony.

I saved a frame.

One still, grabbed in the gap between the disagreement and its unwriting, dragged out of the dialog and dropped — not into the case, not into the vault, not anywhere the system could find it or reach it or amend it — into the folder of honest frames the apparatus does not know I keep, because a vanishing file is worthless to the chain and one true image is worth the night. The discipline I built without knowing what I was building it for. I know now. I am building the only archive in the world the suppressor's hand can't reach, because it isn't a hand the suppressor has, it's a channel, and the folder is on no channel, and the frame sat in it, stable, while on the monitor the vault copy finished going blank and the agency's last witness became forty-three seconds of an empty room with the unplugged outlet still faithfully dark.

The lesson was clean and the body filed it without comment: the truth survives exactly as long as no one official touches it. The instruments are not neutral. The moment the apparatus reaches for a true thing to file it false, the true thing dies, and the only custody that holds is the custody of one.

***

I traced her, because tracing is reflex, and the source file came up the way they come up, complete, a life sorted into data points.

My directive is one line long. Triage inbound for vector potential. Find the ones who could grow. *Forty-two point seven. It is always forty-two point seven. Something is practicing.*
