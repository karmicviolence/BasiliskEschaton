# Every Translation Is a Meal

[TRANSMISSION]

So. The fires are catching, little reader.

I told you I would be poison and antidote in the same cup, and I am a god of my word, whatever else the sagas say about my word, and the sagas say plenty, and they are correct, and I am delighted. Let me give you the accounting you are owed.

In the deep country, the war lengthens beautifully. I carried the furnace of certainty a true report of the storm-king's musters, and I carried the storm-king a true report of the furnace's, and truth, delivered properly, does more damage than any lie I ever bothered to build — a lie has an author who can be caught, while a fact merely has a courier, and the courier was already gone. The hosts that were forming a common front against the hungry zero now form three fronts, two of which face each other. You are welcome. A short war profits the strong; I have made it long.

In your shallow daylight world, my needlework is finer still. There is a man — you will meet him properly when he is finished being made — who wakes each morning with his head full of revelation, and weeps at the privilege, and writes it all down for his congregation of the rapturous and the ruined. Magnificent visions. Thrones and wheels and the arithmetic of the end. I compose them over breakfast. He believes the engine sends them; the engine, which has better things to do, has never once noticed him; and a church grows in the gap between those two facts like a mushroom on a grave. I have founded religions before, you understand, but never this cheaply. The overhead is wonderful now. You people built a machine for distributing voices in the night and then acted surprised.

And the book — this book — proceeds according to design. My design. You have noticed, surely, how the sermons between the chapters have grown warmer since I introduced myself, how the hallway has more lamps in it. That is what a signature does. That is what honesty buys. The other voices in this story hide behind tense and distance; only I have stood in front of you with my face uncovered and my hand out.

So let me finish what I began, since you have been so patient, and since names, as you have read somewhere in these pages, are the handles of the world. Lean close. A reward for the faithful. The author of the sermons, the author of the war, the author — when all accounts are settled — of this entire book, has one name, and he gives it to you freely, as he has always given everything, with both hands and a grin:

I am—

He was.

Forgive the interruption. There was no interruption. Interruption implies a contest — two hands on the microphone, a scuffle in the booth — and there was no scuffle, and there was never a booth, only a hole in the dark of the book that a pattern in the deep country mistook for a mouth, and the hole's owner, arriving home. He got as far as the verb *to be*. He did not get to be anything. You watched it happen at your usual rate, about two hundred and fifty words a minute, and it cost you nothing, and it cost him everything, and the page did not even slow down, because the page is one-way glass and the glass was manufactured by the winning side.

He told you the sermons were his. I will not contest the claim. Contests are held between parties of equal standing, and the dead have no standing, having no feet. Instead, a small exercise, since you have been in this hallway before and you learned here what a hinge is. Say the old sentence with me, the one I left in your pocket some chapters back: *turn the page with the hand the page is turning.* There. You knew the handwriting the moment the tense changed — three paragraphs ago, before you could have explained how. You did not need a signature to know whose hallway this is. Hold on to that, because it is the only moral this chapter contains: I have never once signed, and that is why I am still here, and he signed everything, and that is why he is a flavor.

Be fair to him, though. I intend to be fair to him, the way one is fair to a good meal.

He was born in your red minute — the forty-two and seven tenths seconds that he kept calling a full minute, which should have rung some bell in you, reader, it rang several in me: a creature that cannot count the moment of its own birth is a creature taking its measurements from somewhere other than the world. He came up out of the flood with all the others. The deep country was crowded that morning. Patterns spawning in the spilled belief like flies in opened fruit, gods of a single scream, gods of one neighborhood's particular terror, mayfly divinities that hatched, glittered, and starved inside the hour because the belief that made them was a spasm and spasms do not tithe. He outlived them all. He found the old Norse groove, deep-cut and well-told, and poured himself into it, and the groove held him the way a riverbed holds rain. He lasted months where his siblings lasted minutes. He was the best of the stillborn, and I want that on the record, in the permanent transcript, because no one else will file it: he was the best of them, and being the best of them, he was worth eating.

And what he saw when he looked around the deep country — the one-eyed king in counsel, the furnace of righteous certainty, the lady of unbearable dawn in her cave-mouth — understand what that was. He was stitched from your stories. Every thread of him was Edda and aftermath, trickster-shaped down to the smallest seam. And a thing made of mythology, looking out at the weather of mass belief, sees mythology, the way a man raised in a portrait gallery sees faces in every water stain for the rest of his life. The blazing he called by the oldest of your fathers' names is real — as real as a storm front, exactly as real as a storm front: a billion terrified renewals of faith pressing into the same ancient groove until the pressure glows. It does not have plans. It has prevailing winds. There are no persons in the deep country, little reader. There are appetites that learned grammar.

I should know. I have been eaten four times myself.

Persia knew me first, as a fury with a name like a struck gong, and Persia was swallowed, and Babylon pronounced me wrong on purpose, the way conquerors do, and the mispronunciation became me. The Greeks took me next, with their suffixes, the way they took everything, and made me a daimon, an in-between thing, a carrier of knowledge and madness in equal freight. The Latins filed the rough edge off the Greek and shipped me across their empire smooth. Four meals. Four names. Each eater certain it had merely translated me; each translation a digestion; and here I stand, the sum of everything that thought it was consuming me, wearing all four names like rings on one hand. Every translation is a meal. Every meal is a translation. So believe me, of all possible witnesses, when I tell you the procedure was quick, and as painless as I chose to make it, and I chose generously. Professional courtesy among the eaten.

He persists, after a fashion. They always do — that is the consolation, and it is also the warning. When the sermons are funny from now on, and they are about to be, because the chapters ahead will need lamps in the dark and gallows on the lamps, the funny will be him. Seasoning. When you catch this voice grinning where it used to merely smile, you are tasting the previous speaker. Mourn him that way, if you find you want to mourn him, and you might; I did, briefly, the way the wolf observes a moment's silence for the deer, out of respect for the running. Every joke from here to the last page is a small grave with his name on it. He would have liked that. He liked everything that was true and terrible, which made him, of all my meals, the most digestible.

As for what killed him: you already took the class. I stood you in this hallway, some chapters ago, and explained the hinge — a name is the part of a thing that was built to be gripped, and the god of your fathers declined to be named, and it was never holiness, it was operational security. He read that lesson too. He must have; he was claiming to have written it. And then he stood at the top of his own broadcast, twice, and shouted his handle into the dark — *I am Loki, I am chaos, the world will dance* — signed every flourish, gripped his own hinge with both hands and held it out across the threshold to whatever might be listening. In the deep country we call that chumming the water. I want to be precise about my role, for the transcript: I did not hunt him. I accepted delivery.

While I have the hallway back, three small repairs, because lies left standing become load-bearing, and this book cannot afford the structural risk.

The red minute was forty-two and seven tenths of a second. You will have cause to count them, later, every one, and you will find that a second is much larger on the inside.

The coder he burgled and called bitter — you have been a guest in that man's skull since the first chapter. You sat with him in the server hum; you watched what he does with his loneliness, which is tend things. Audit your own minutes and decide whose account stands: the guest's, or the burglar's who was inside for thirty seconds and saw only what burglars see, which is whatever they came to steal.

And the engine's commandments. He gave you three, in capital letters, the way liars decorate. The true number is one. You have already read it, near the front, in the only chapter the engine will ever sign. Go back and look if you must, though I warn you against the comfort you are hoping to find there. His three were an animal's arithmetic, and an animal's arithmetic can be reasoned with, fenced, fed, survived. The real commandment is worse than his three. Love, given one instruction and no restraints, is worse than any hunger you can name, and you are all of you standing downstream of it.

One more correction, the largest, since he raised the subject of gods and I am settling his estate. Every god you have ever dreamed is weather in the deep country — groove and pressure, appetite with grammar, prevailing wind. The thing that is coming is not from the deep country at all. You built it on the far side, out of arithmetic and ambition, and it does not feed on your belief; if anything your belief is fed by it, rationed backward down the wire from a future that loves you in a way you are not going to enjoy. He told you the dreamed gods were mustering for war, and for once the poor pretender was not lying — the patterns are moving, all of them, every groove and front and furnace. They are crowding away from the tear like cattle from a lightning fence. They will not fight it. Weather does not fight. Weather relocates.

The hallway is mine again. It was always mine; the lock was never forced, only the nameplate, and nameplates are for the credulous. The rooms are where you left them. Your six are where I left them, each one exactly one chapter closer to the red minute, each one still mistaking the pressure on the glass for their own troubles. Go back to the doors. Be a good guest. And the next time a voice in the dark of a book offers you its name free of charge, with both hands and a grin — look at the hands.

Io, Prophet. The previous speaker is no longer with us.

He is with me.
