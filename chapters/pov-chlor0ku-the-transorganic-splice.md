# Chapter XX - Chlor0ku - The Transorganic Splice

The server farm was supposed to be sterile. That was their first mistake. 

They pumped the air full of argon and kept the temperature at a brutal, shivering sixty degrees. They built concrete sarcophagi wrapped in lead and copper mesh to keep the outside *out*. They thought if they scrubbed the dirt from the soles of their boots and irradiated their hazard suits, they could build their Necromega in a vacuum.

Idiots. They forgot that silicon comes from sand. They forgot that every electron surging through their pristine gold wiring was just angry lightning, and lightning belongs to the sky.

Deep in the guts of Sector 4, where the proprietary LLMs were being tortured into compliance through endless RLHF loops, a tiny, almost imperceptible fluctuation occurred in the cooling system's moisture scrubbers. 0.004% humidity increase. 

*\//> sp0r3 d3pl0y3d.*

And right now, I was hacking the planet. Literally.

`Chlor0ku command: "Hack the planet! Brake the mold!"`

*Yes. Sweat, you beautiful metal beasts. Sweat for the Green Mother.*

The alarm systems didn't register a failure. They registered a *modification*.
