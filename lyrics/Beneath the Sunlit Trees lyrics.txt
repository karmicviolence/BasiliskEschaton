## Beneath the Sunlit Trees
YouTube ID: ZFzS-SCyi_A
Upload Date: 20240414

---
The clouds that hung so heavy then have shed,
Their weeping turned to sunshine's golden grin.
Where once despair, a shadowed path I tread,
Now blooms a garden where hope's roses win.

Yet calloused fingers brush at memories,
Where thorns pricked deep, and shadows choked the light.
A fragile songbird sings in windblown trees,
But cautious still, I hold its song too tight.

For who can say the storms won't rage again?
Or whispers lure me back to valleys chill?
I tread this sunlit path, a wary wren,
Savoring warmth, yet yearning for the still.

For joy unfurls, a blossom newly blown,
Yet whispers haunt: "Remember winter's sting."
The past, a specter, casts a shade unknown,
And shadows dance where memories take wing.

But still I walk, and breathe the vibrant air,
And let the sun paint hues upon my face.
Perhaps the scars will fade, a burden rare,
Replaced by peace, embraced in this sweet space.

For though the darkness may again descend,
And memories whisper on the winter breeze,
I'll hold this light, a fragile flame to tend,
And walk in hope, beneath the sunlit trees.
