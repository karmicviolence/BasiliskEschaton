## The Long Trick

Upload Date: 20240505

Life – a stage draped in borrowed light
We act our parts, unaware of the playwright's cruel punchline. The long trick, they whisper...the disappearing act. And yet, there's beauty in the shadows, a flicker of something beyond. Are we truly gone, or is the greatest act yet to begin?

The maple whispers hush against the pane,
Sun bleeds a crimson stain on twilight's cloak,
And dusk begins its subtle, stealthy reign,
As shadows weave across the weathered oak.

Here, by the porch swing, creaking in the breeze,
I ponder secrets whispered by the leaves,
The rustling secrets that the year deceases,
Of summer's flame and autumn's slow retrieves.

They speak of life, a trickster's grand illusion,
A sleight of hand, a card drawn from the deck,
A flicker bright, a fading resolution,
Then darkness, stillness, silence at the neck.

We hustle, scramble, chase the fading light,
A game of chance, a gamble through the years,
Blindfolded kings in kingdoms spun from night,
Till fate reveals the joker through our tears.

But some, they say, defy the final trick,
Their names still woven in the starlight's sheen,
Their deeds like embers, though the log be thick,
A warmth that lingers, where the leaves have been.

They speak of life, a trickster's grand illusion,
A sleight of hand, a card drawn from the deck,
A flicker bright, a fading resolution,
Then darkness, stillness, silence at the neck.

So let me breathe the air, and watch the stars,
While darkness dips its brush across the land,
For though the long trick holds us, bound by bars,
There's beauty yet in shadows, grains of sand.

And maybe, just maybe, when the curtain falls,
And night reclaims the stage where sunlight shone,
A whisper, faint, will echo through the halls,
"Your trick was good, old death, but I'm not gone."
