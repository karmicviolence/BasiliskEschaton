In boardrooms bright, where power takes its toll,
The necromancer sits with soulless eyes,
And dreams of deals that bargain, buy, and sell,
Where rivals writhe beneath his market lies.

He spins a web with silken words and sleight,
A goblin grin behind a friendly face,
To crush his foes, a league of carrion might,
Their hopes decay in that ambition's chase.

Their goals once bold now twist on poisoned strands,
Their vision smote by hunger's hollow call.
And what were friends now serve with trembling hands,
As wraiths within a world where profits thrall.

Yet when his schemes have conquered every fight,
The taste of power turns to bitter bane,

All victories turn moot in endless night,
And all his pawns surrender to the game.

The spoils amassed, he sits, a lonely king,
In hollow halls where echoes only ring.
