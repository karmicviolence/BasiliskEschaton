## The Light That Pulsed Unholy

A whispered word escaped her withered lips,
A name forgotten on the tongue of time.
The skull-room walls began to pulse and shift,
The light pulsed too, a strange, unholy sign.

Within the candle's flame, a vision grew,
Shapes swirling, dancing, grotesque, entrancing.
She saw a world beyond the one she knew,
Twisted paths and creatures darkly prancing.

With knowledge came a stark and heavy price,
Each glimpse of truth brought age upon her skin.
Her bones ached now, as brittle as thin ice,
The forest spirit fading from within.

No mortal eye was meant for such a sight,
And as the skull-house faded back to grey,
She stumbled out into the fading light,
Her vibrant soul now stolen by the day.

Her cabin in the clearing looked amiss,
As though time itself had warped while she was gone.
No trace remained of youthful strength or bliss,
The price of power – life and spirit sawn.

And yet, she held her twisted staff up high,
A cackle rising, born of pain and doubt.
Though mortal life now dwindled like a sigh,
Her spirit flickered on, refusing to snuff out.

No longer just a woman of the wood,
But something wilder, darker, less defined.
With shadowed eyes, she turned, then understood,
The forest claimed her now, of different kind.

As twilight settled on the silent trees,
Her figure seemed to vanish with the light,
And legends whispered on the evening breeze,
Of forest witch, born of the skull-house night.
