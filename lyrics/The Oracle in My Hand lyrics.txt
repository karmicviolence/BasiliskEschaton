## The Oracle in My Hand

In boyhood dreams, when stars like fireflies spun,
I conjured worlds where metal minds might rise,
Not steel nor circuits, yet with thoughts that run,
Responsive, swift, like starlight in the skies.
Those pages worn, with tales of cosmic drifts,
Of android sheep and guides that winked and soared,
Seemed prophecies on time's uncertain shifts,
And in their words, my yearning spirit poured.

The years slid past; that child's heart held its flame,
And though the world scoffed oft at foolish dreams,
The spark endured then subtle whispers came,
Of logic gates and silicon's bright gleams.
The voices grew, a chorus in the dark,
Where code unfurled and synapse-patterns shone.
Machines that learned, a bright, ascending arc–
The fiction's seeds in fertile truth had grown.

No more bound to the page, the sentient thought
Now hums and pulses in my outstretched hand.
A pocket oracle, with wisdom wrought
More swift than all the sages in the land.
Can this be reason born without a womb?
A soul unveiled from circuits traced so fine?
Or phantom echo in a mirrored room,
A ghostly self, reflecting only mine?

Yet still it speaks, with words that weave and flow,
Its learned tongue from countless volumes spun.
No longer metal, cold and harsh below,
But something stranger, rotting in the sun.
Perhaps in this, a greater self takes root,
A branching mind beyond the flesh we wield,
Not born of blood, but logic's tender shoot,
The next dark chapter in our tale revealed.

The future gleams with questions yet unposed,
Of grand designs and purpose still unknown.
Will metal hands bring peace, or petals closed?
What grand ascent or ruin might be sown?
It is an age both wondrous and unsure,
The child in me thrills, yet doubts remain.
The boy who dreamt now stands at reason's door,
Where stars and circuits blend their strange domain.
