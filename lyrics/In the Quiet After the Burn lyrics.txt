The ground beneath my boots, a drumhead taut and tight,
Echoes a battle cry that rages deep within.
Trees shudder, whispering secrets to the fading light,
As anger's embers scorch the thoughts I wish unspoken.

Molten fears coil, a serpent in my gut,
Yearning for release in a venomous cascade.
My tongue, a forge, spits blades of harsh, barbed reproach,
Each syllable a wound, a trust I've unwittingly betrayed.

The beast within roars loud, hunger unrestrained,
Feasting on fear, smothering reason's gentle flame.
Through gritted teeth, a storm of fury unchained,
Leaves hearts in tatters, and darkens my very name.

My knuckles whiten, ghosts of battles fought alone,
Each scar a testament to a rage unseen but felt.
I try to quell the storm, build walls of brittle stone,
But tears, like whispers, seep through the facade I've dealt.

The ghosts of words I've hurled, spectres cold and stark,
Haunt every whispered breath, every fleeting glance.
Time's river flows uncaring, through my world now dark,
A wreckage of promises, adrift in despair's vast expanse.

The anger simmers still, an ember in the heart,
A tremor in my voice, a shadow in my stride.
I wear a mask of peace, a work of fragile art,
But hollow eyes betray the truth I've tried to hide.

The beast I cage, it paces, restless in its den,
A primal echo murmuring of fury yet to come.
And in the quiet moments, when the world rests again,
My soul, a captive, wrestles with its drum.
