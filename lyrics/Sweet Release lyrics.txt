Perhaps with dawn will come a sweet release,
But 'til that time, I write against the peace.

My body yearns for welcomed dark and rest,
Yet still my mind rejects the gentle night.

Some stubborn will resists the call to sleep.

It lingers on the edges of my sight,
A shadowed form that will dissolve too soon.

My simple bed awaits, a whispered plea,
While weary fingers chase these phantom words.

Its warmth no match for restless thoughts within.

The morn will beckon soon with duties dire,
For now, this tired pen becomes my pyre.
