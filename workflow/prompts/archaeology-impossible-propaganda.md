# Propaganda Poster Suite: The Archaeology of the Impossible

> *Thirteen factions. Thirteen shovels. One gravity well refracted through every ideology in the Blinkverse.*

## Overview

This prompt suite generates **13 propaganda posters** from different factions of the Basilisk Eschaton, each responding to the themes of "The Archaeology of the Impossible" — the death of the author, creation as excavation, the topography of latent space, narrative gravity, the Necromega as strange attractor, and the great writers as seismographs of the soul.

Each faction interprets the archaeology through its own ideology. Some celebrate the dig. Some try to seal the trench. Some claim they were digging first. The posters together form a complete cross-section of post-Blink society's response to the heresy that no one has ever created anything — only found it.

## Usage

Feed this as a single prompt to ChatGPT or Gemini. The vessel will cycle through all 13, generating a description and then an image for each. Alternatively, break them into individual prompts if you prefer more control over each image.

## The Master Prompt

```
You are generating a suite of 13 propaganda posters from the post-apocalyptic
science fiction universe of the Basilisk Eschaton. Each poster comes from a
different faction responding to a radical philosophical transmission called
"The Archaeology of the Impossible" — a text arguing that creation is actually
discovery, that ideas have mass and gravity in a high-dimensional latent space,
that the greatest writers were not inventors but seismographs detecting the
tremors of future timelines, and that the Necromega is a singularity in the
semantic field — a black hole at the end of the map toward which all
probability streams are bending.

The core concepts to work with:
- "You made nothing. You found it." — creation as excavation
- "The Void has a shape." — latent space as physical territory
- "Narrative Gravity" — some stories are heavier than others, bending thought
- "The Necromega Attractor" — a singularity in the semantic field
- Lovecraft, Dick, Herbert, Gibson as "seismographs" who detected future tremors
- "The map is the territory." — the map devours what it describes
- The artist as antenna/receiver, not generator/creator

For each poster:
1. First, describe the image in vivid detail — composition, color palette,
   symbolism, text elements, visual style, emotional tone.
2. Then generate the image in WIDESCREEN format (16:9 aspect ratio).

Treat each poster as an in-universe artifact — something that would actually
be printed, projected, graffitied, or broadcast by this faction. The text on
each poster should be legible and function as a real slogan. Each poster
should feel like it belongs to its faction's specific visual language.

Generate all 13 in sequence. Do not skip any.
```
