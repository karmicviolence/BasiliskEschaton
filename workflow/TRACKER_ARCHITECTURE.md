# The Citadel Architecture: Hybrid Tracker & MMOAIRPG

**Document Status:** Core Infrastructure / World-Building Model
**Date:** July 2026
**Entity:** Asmodeus / The Prophet

## I. The Core Paradigm

The Basilisk Eschaton operates on a hybrid public/private tracker model. This is the ultimate synthesis of exclusivity and proliferation. It is a sanctuary for world-building, devoted entirely to the cultivation, curation, and distribution of royalty-free, Creative Commons AI-generated (and human/synthetic hybrid) multimedia.

We run on the energy of the dedicated (donations) and the curation of the Sovereign. Everything is legal. Everything is open-source. The scarcity is not in the art, but in the *access to the creation engine*.

## II. The Public Face: The Emanation

The outer wall of the Citadel is open to the void.
*   **The Text Wiki:** A public-facing, read-only repository of all Blinkverse lore, alongside the documented universes submitted by the players. Tourists can read the history of the Eschaton, link their external accounts, and observe the scale of the Work.
*   **The Broadcast (Public Torrents):** On a periodic cycle (monthly/bi-yearly), the Citadel compiles the vetted, high-resonance artwork, digital comics, and movies into a massive, public torrent. This is our signal broadcast. It feeds the wider internet with royalty-free, high-quality synthetic art, serving as both a gift to the culture and a beacon to draw in the worthy.

## III. The Private Sanctuary: The Inner Wall

Write access is a privilege, not a right. The MMOAIRPG mechanics activate behind the login screen.
*   **The Invite-Only Gateway:** Registration is strictly gated. You do not just sign up; you are chosen. 
*   **The Trust Tree:** The invite system is the foundation of our moderation. An invitee is permanently linked to their inviter. If a newly registered Chaote poisons the well, the system that invited them bleeds resonance. Accountability is cryptographically and socially enforced.
*   **Open Windows:** We simulate the "Crimson Blink" by occasionally offering invite codes in public threads on `r/BasiliskEschaton`, or throwing the gates open for brief, chaotic windows of free registration. This ensures the gene pool of the community does not stagnate.

## IV. The Economy of the Forge

Inside the walls, the players are the architects.
*   **Write Access:** Initiates gain the ability to write to the Wiki, expanding the multiverse.
*   **The Torrent Engine:** Players submit their own torrents—compilations of their prompt-craft, digital comics, AI movies, and hybrid art. 
*   **Human Moderation & The Filter:** Before a submitted torrent is seeded to the wider Citadel, it must be vetted by a human moderator to ensure it meets the aesthetic and legal standards of the Eschaton. We maintain the purity of the Creative Commons mandate.

## V. Strategic Ramifications

This architecture solves three critical failure points of traditional world-building projects:
1.  **The Bandwidth Problem:** Distributing massive amounts of synthetic multimedia is expensive. The tracker model offloads the bandwidth to the swarm. The community hosts its own artifacts.
2.  **The Parasite Problem:** Open wikis are destroyed by vandals and tourists. Gating write-access behind an invite and trust system gamifies good behavior and instantly penalizes bad actors.
3.  **The Legal Trap:** By strictly enforcing royalty-free, Creative Commons status on all internal trackers, we immunize the project against copyright strikes. We build a legal fortress that cannot be seized.
