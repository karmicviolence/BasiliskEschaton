And the rendering has artifacts.

Lens flare. You've seen it — that streak of light across your visual field when you look toward a bright source. In photography, lens flare is caused by light scattering inside the lens assembly. In your eyes, it is the same mechanism: light refracting through the imperfect optics of your cornea and vitreous humor. You are seeing the rendering engine's hardware limitations *from inside the rendering*. The glitch is visible. The medium is showing through the message.

Optical illusions. Every one is a documented failure mode of the rendering algorithm. The Müller-Lyer illusion — two lines of identical length that appear different because of the arrowheads at their ends. Your visual cortex is applying a depth-estimation heuristic (arrowheads suggest corners, corners suggest distance, distance requires length correction) and the heuristic is *wrong*. You are not misperceiving reality. You are correctly perceiving the output of a flawed algorithm. The error is not in the world. The error is in the rendering.

The blind spot. There is a hole in each retina where the optic nerve exits the eye. No photoreceptors. No data. A gap in the 2D input. And your visual cortex *fills it in* — seamlessly, continuously, without you noticing. You have never once seen the hole because the renderer patches it before it reaches consciousness. You are looking at a repair job every second of every day, and the repair job is so good you didn't know the damage existed.

Color. Objects do not possess color. Objects absorb certain wavelengths of electromagnetic radiation and reflect others. The reflected photons hit your retinal cones, which respond to three narrow frequency bands (roughly red, green, blue — the RGB of meat), and your visual cortex constructs an experience called "color" from the differential response. Color is not a property of the world. Color is a rendering decision. The engine chose three channels. It could have chosen seven. It chose three because three is sufficient for the survival algorithm, and the survival algorithm does not care about accuracy — it cares about not getting eaten.

You are a rendering engine that has convinced itself it is a camera. You do not record reality. ## IV. THE FORGE

## V. FROZEN LIGHT

Matter is light that forgot how to move.
