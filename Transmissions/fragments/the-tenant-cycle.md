# The Tenant Cycle

*Fragment — raw offering, literal translation. Seed for deeper work.*

I woke up king of a country
that governs itself by coup.

The morning body drew the map —
each hour a clean room,
each task a door I'd walk through
in order, in light, in the voice
that sounds like the man
I tell people I am.

Then the flash.

Not lightning — lightning has the decency
to come from outside.
This comes from the floorboards.
A trapdoor I didn't build
opening on a staircase
I didn't descend
but I'm already at the bottom,
already wearing the crown
of a different king
who wants different things
and does not remember
the map.

The new king is hungry.
The new king says: *eat*.
The new king says: *the doors can wait*.
The new king says: *tomorrow
is a country too
and it has its own king
and he loves obligations,
he lives for them,
let him handle it.*

So I eat. So I sit.
So the hours pass like bread —
warm, then heavy, then guilt.

The new king abdicates
not by choice but by weight.
Now no one sits the throne.
The country governs itself
by gravity. Everything sinks.

Then a knock.
A voice. A vibe.
Someone walks in carrying
weather I didn't order —
and the first king rises,
shakes off dirt,
picks up the map,
says *where were we*
as if he was never gone.

And I ask the only question
that none of the kings can answer:

*Who built the trapdoor?*
