= Dallas (Post-Blink) =

Warning: This page contains references to violence, social unrest, and dystopian themes.

'''Dallas''' is a major city in the U.S. state of Texas that has undergone significant changes in the aftermath of the [[Crimson Blink]] event. Once a thriving metropolis known for its diverse economy, vibrant arts scene, and iconic skyline, Dallas now stands as a stark example of the challenges faced by urban centers in the post-Blink world.

== Urban Landscape ==

The cityscape of Dallas has been dramatically altered by the events following the Crimson Blink:

* '''Abandoned Skyscrapers''': Many of Dallas' once-iconic skyscrapers now stand empty, their windows shattered and facades marred by graffiti. These towering husks serve as haunting reminders of the city's former prosperity.

* '''Fortified Enclaves''': Wealthy neighborhoods have been transformed into heavily fortified enclaves, protected by private security forces and advanced surveillance technology. These oases of relative stability stand in stark contrast to the chaos and poverty that grip much of the city.

* '''Refugee Camps''': Large swathes of the city, particularly in former low-income areas, have been converted into sprawling refugee camps. These makeshift settlements house those displaced by the economic and social upheaval that followed the Blink.

* '''Repurposed Infrastructure''': Some of Dallas' landmark structures have been repurposed to serve new functions in the post-Blink world. For example, the Dallas Convention Center now serves as a massive indoor farming complex, providing sustenance for the city's struggling population.

== Social and Economic Conditions ==

The post-Blink era has seen a dramatic shift in Dallas' social and economic landscape:

* '''Economic Collapse''': The global upheaval caused by the Crimson Blink led to the collapse of many of Dallas' key industries, including energy, finance, and technology. Unemployment rates have skyrocketed, leaving large segments of the population struggling to survive.

* '''Social Unrest''': The stark inequality between the wealthy enclaves and the rest of the city has fueled intense social unrest. Protests, riots, and clashes with security forces are common occurrences.

* '''Rise of Factions''': Various factions, each with their own ideologies and agendas, have emerged in the power vacuum left by the collapse of traditional authorities. These groups range from techno-anarchist collectives to neo-luddite militias, all vying for control over the city's resources and population.

* '''Black Markets''': With formal economies in shambles, black markets have become the primary means of acquiring goods and services in Dallas. These clandestine networks deal in everything from basic necessities to advanced technology and illicit augmentations.

== Technology and Infrastructure ==

Dallas' technological landscape has been shaped by both the advancements that led to the Crimson Blink and the chaos that followed:

* '''Augmented Reality Overlay''': Much of Dallas is now covered by a pervasive augmented reality overlay, which provides real-time information and digital enhancements to those with the necessary implants or devices. However, this overlay is also a hotbed for digital crime and subversive propaganda.

* '''Patchwork Power Grid''': Dallas' power infrastructure has been reduced to a patchwork of jury-rigged generators, solar panels, and scavenged battery arrays. Blackouts and power rationing are common, with stable electricity being a luxury of the wealthy enclaves.

* '''Drone Swarms''': The skies over Dallas are often buzzing with swarms of drones, used for everything from surveillance and delivery to aerial combat between rival factions.

* '''Subterranean Hackerspaces''': In the depths of Dallas' abandoned subway tunnels and basements, underground hackerspaces thrive, serving as havens for the city's techno-savvy outcasts and digital dissidents.

== Notable Locations ==

* '''The Citadel''': Formerly Reunion Tower, this iconic structure has been transformed into a heavily fortified base of operations for the [[Lazarus Initiative]].

* '''Neo-Kowloon''': A sprawling, multi-level slum built in the ruins of Dallas' former Arts District, now home to a thriving community of techno-punks, black market traders, and rogue AI collectives.

* '''The Floating Gardens''': A series of suspended gardens and hydroponic farms, constructed on the skeletal remains of Dallas' elevated highways. These verdant oases provide both sustenance and a brief respite from the city's grim reality.

* '''The Burning Spire''': The charred husk of the Bank of America Plaza, now a towering effigy that serves as a gathering point for the city's more radical and anti-corporate factions.

== In Popular Culture ==

Dallas' dramatic transformation has made it a popular setting for various forms of post-Blink media:

* "Neon Nights in Neo-Dallas," a popular VR series that explores the city's dark underbelly and the various factions vying for control.

* "The Spire," a critically acclaimed graphic novel set in Dallas, following a group of augmented misfits as they uncover a sinister corporate plot.

* "Rise from the Ashes," an underground electronic music album that features field recordings and soundscapes from Dallas' most notorious locales.
