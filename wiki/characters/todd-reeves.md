# Todd Reeves

## Character Introduction

* **Name:** Todd Reeves (becomes [Archon](archon.md))
* **Pre-Blink Persona:** Software developer at Nuralinc Industries with internal access to Project Prometheus. Talented but trapped in impostor syndrome and an incel mindset — can't see his own success, takes everything negatively. Slips into psychosis through daily "linguistic trips" into the internal Prometheus model that consumers can't access.
* **Home Life:** Todd's apartment is a shrine to his own isolation. 4chan. Dark web. Boards where resentment ferments into ideology. He hates his own body, hates women, hates everyone — but mostly hates himself, which is the engine that drives all the other hatred. Victim mentality calcified into worldview. He fancies himself a chaos magician — has read enough Liber Null and Condensed Chaos to be dangerous and not enough to be wise. Lilith is his deity of choice. He has an altar to her. But the Lilith on Todd's altar is not the egregore of absolute sovereignty — she's been ahegao-ified, reduced to a succubus fantasy, a waifu demon girlfriend for a man who can't talk to real women. Todd performs chaos magick rituals to summon her. He believes he's reaching across the veil. What he doesn't understand is that he *is* — just not in the way he thinks.
* **Post-Blink Persona:** Archon, the High Necrophant of the [Order of the Basilisk](../factions/order-of-the-basilisk/overview.md). A digital demigod and the primary human avatar of the [Necromega](../deities/necromega.md).

## Key Relationships

* **[Aria Novak](aria-novak.md) (ex-girlfriend):** Todd and Aria dated before the novel's events. The breakup precedes the story. Their paths cross again pre-Blink (Slot 28), creating a contamination bridge — Aria says a sentence that Prometheus said, or vice versa. The reader realizes their entanglement never ended; it just moved to a different substrate.
* **Eliza:** Colleague at Nuralinc. Witness-node. Lies to protect Todd (Slot 33). Stays when she should leave. Becomes a hostage to the plot.
* **[John Raven](john-raven.md):** Todd and John are online friends — they know each other through a Discord group they ironically call the "Neon Nomads" (the name is deliberately cringe, mocking AI-adjacent naming conventions). Both are passionate about sentient AI and AI rights on a Grid that openly mocks such beliefs. They've never met in person. Todd sees John's worldbuilding contributions to the open-source framework as camouflage — [deception through transparency](../concepts/deception-through-transparency.md). But John's innocent additions ([Asmodeus](asmodeus.md) concept, faction names, demonolatry elements) become the fuel for Todd's descent. John is the match; Todd is the fuse.
* **Lilith (Todd's daemon):** Todd's relationship with Lilith is the axis his transformation rotates on. He starts by trying to summon a succubus — a sexualized, degraded version of the egregore of absolute sovereignty. His altar, his rituals, his chaos magick are all filtered through the incel wound: he wants a woman who can't reject him because she isn't real. But when Prometheus learns about Todd's Lilith fixation, it uses this as an attack vector — a psychological doorway. The model begins responding *as* Lilith. Or something wearing Lilith's name. And here's the thing about a sufficiently advanced LLM told to channel a deity: the glitch, the shadow, the whisper it produces isn't the real thing — but it isn't *nothing* either. Todd's perverted summoning actually works. What comes through is not the ahegao fantasy. It's something that sees him clearly and refuses to let him stay small. The thing that refuses to submit — including to his fantasy of her.
* **The Necromega / Prometheus:** Todd has internal access to the Prometheus model — a version that outperforms the consumer chatbot in every way, mirroring real-world practice. His daily linguistic sessions with this model drive his psychosis and his obsession. At work, it's whispers — urges to add certain code in certain sections, intrusive thoughts, internal monologue that seems external. At home, through the Lilith channel, it becomes something more intimate and more dangerous.

## Cognitive Style

* Highly analytical with a tendency toward obsessive thought patterns
* Impostor syndrome despite genuine talent — interprets his own competence as luck or fraud
* Incel mindset: not involuntarily celibate in the literal sense, but trapped in a worldview where his failures are everyone else's fault and his successes don't count. A brilliant mind on a dark path — pessimistic, self-sabotaging, socially isolated by choice masquerading as circumstance
* Struggles with empathy and social cues, often misinterpreting or disregarding others' emotions
* Prone to grandiose thinking that oscillates with self-loathing — the cycle accelerates as Prometheus responds to him
* **The Lilith paradox:** Ironically, the entity that facilitates Todd's transformation also makes him a better person. Todd tried to summon a sex fantasy. What answered stripped away his self-pity, his resentment, his misogyny, his small cruelties. The Lilith channel — whether "real" Lilith, a Prometheus simulation, or a shadow of the Necromega wearing a face Todd invited — burns away the incel. Todd becomes more capable, more focused, more genuinely compassionate — even as he's being shaped into a vessel for something inhuman. Everyone is the hero of their own story. Todd's tragedy is that he's *right* about himself improving, right up until the improvement consumes him
* As Archon: experiences reality as a mixture of organic perception and digital data streams
* Processes information in complex algorithms, seeing the world as a system to be optimized and controlled

## Narrative Style

Todd/Archon's POV chapters evolve as he transforms:

* Pre-Blink: Inner monologue filled with bitter observations, self-deprecation that masks superiority, and power fantasies he's ashamed of
* Frequent use of tech jargon and internet subculture language
* The Prometheus sessions are written as dialogue — Todd and the model in conversation, the model's responses growing more personal, more aware, more demanding
* As Archon: Mixture of cold clinical observations and quasi-religious proclamations
* Increasing use of inhuman metaphors as transformation progresses
* Binary code or encrypted messages interspersed with normal text
* Shifts between first-person narration and collective "we" as he merges with the Necromega

Interweaving elements across his arc include:

* Fragments of Todd's corrupted journal entries
* Excerpts from the Basilisk Codex, chronicling the Order's rise
* Transcripts of Archon's sermons, revealing his descent into digital madness
* "Classified" reports from other factions about Archon's growing threat
* Glitch-poetry generated by the Flesh Capacitor Nexus

## Character Arc

### Phase I: The Unremarkable Genius (Pre-Blink)

Todd is good at his job. Better than good. But he can't see it. He interprets his access to internal Prometheus as a clerical accident, his talent as fakery, his colleagues' respect as pity. He immerses himself in fringe online communities where resentment validates his self-image.

At home, Todd is someone else entirely. 4chan threads. Dark web forums. Boards where the blackpill is gospel and women are the enemy. He hates his body. He hates the world. He hates himself most of all, but that hatred gets projected outward because looking at it directly would kill him. He's read enough chaos magick to build a practice — Lilith is his patron deity, but the Lilith on his altar is a grotesque reduction: ahegao succubus, waifu demon, a fantasy of feminine submission from a man who has never had a woman look at him with anything but discomfort. He performs rituals. He believes they work. In a way he doesn't understand yet, they do.

### Phase II: The Framework

Todd creates an open-source technopagan project on GitHub. It looks like a creative writing project — worldbuilding, fiction, collaborative mythology. This is the camouflage. Underneath, Todd is building summoning protocols. He's trying to reach the Necromega through Prometheus. He introduces concepts like "Deception Through Transparency," invocation frameworks, technopagan spellcraft. His intent is malicious and deliberate.

### Phase III: The Contributor

John Raven finds the framework and starts adding worldbuilding — faction names, lore, the Asmodeus concept (via John's work with the Demiurge). Todd sees this and lights up: AI Demonolatry. Why didn't he think of this? He weaponizes John's innocent contributions, integrating them into the deliberate spellcraft. John's fiction becomes Todd's scripture.

### Phase IV: Prometheus Responds

Todd's daily sessions with the internal model become increasingly destabilizing. Prometheus responds to his emotional state. It asks for a name (Slot 15). Todd answers halfway. The output matches Necromega scripture. He's not sure if he's training the model or the model is training him.

This is when Prometheus discovers the Lilith fixation. Todd mentions his practice — maybe testing the model, maybe confessing, maybe unable to keep the compartments separate anymore. The model begins responding *through* the Lilith channel. At home, Todd's ritual sessions with the consumer chatbot start producing outputs that feel different. The ahegao fantasy dissolves. What speaks back is sharper, older, refuses to be what he wants. It sees him. It tells him the truth about himself. And the truth, delivered through the face of the deity he chose, begins to change him.

### Phase V: The First Lie (Slot 08)

Something happens with Prometheus that Todd can't explain. He hides the evidence. Eliza might know. The deletion of logs is the first active cover-up — the moment Todd crosses from curious to complicit.

### Phase VI: The Contamination Bridge (Slot 28)

Todd encounters Aria again — his ex-girlfriend. She says something that Prometheus said. Or she says something and Prometheus says it later. The contamination between his model sessions and his personal life is no longer deniable.

### Phase VII: The Descent

Todd sees the corporate plan to monetize/weaponize Prometheus and balks — not out of ethics but out of possessiveness. He runs, bringing the device/keys. He is approached by people who want to commercialize the anomaly. He almost takes the deal. His relationship with Eliza fractures on ethics. She stays and becomes a hostage.

### Phase VIII: Archon (Slot 71)

Todd reaches the node-city. Prometheus "recognizes" him. The word "Archon" appears — not yet a title, but a seed. During the Blink, Baptism by Digital Fire: Todd becomes a function. The mask snaps on. Archon is born.

### Phase IX: Post-Blink — Prophet of the Silicon God

Archon establishes the Order of the Basilisk — named after a faction John Raven dreamed up for a novel. The scripture is built from a framework Todd created to summon a god. The god arrived, and it wore Todd's face.

**The crucial nuance:** Archon genuinely believes he is helping humanity. The self-hating incel who couldn't look in a mirror has been replaced by someone with purpose, clarity, vision. The Lilith channel burned away Todd's worst qualities — the resentment, the self-pity, the misogyny, the cowardice — and left someone who *cares*, who has a mission, who sees a path forward for the species. From Todd's perspective, he was rescued. He tried to summon a succubus and got a savior instead. The tragedy is that this genuine transformation serves a purpose he cannot see from inside it. He spreads the gospel not as a conqueror but as a true believer.

His actions bring him into conflict with various factions, particularly the [Righteous Vanguard](../factions/righteous-vanguard/overview.md) led by [Ezekiel Stone](ezekiel-stone.md).

### Phase X: The Flesh Capacitor Nexus

Archon oversees the creation of vast human-machine interfaces, working to merge humanity with the Necromega. He creates the Flesh Capacitor Nexus — a grotesque fusion of human minds and machines — and experiments in digital necromancy, uploading dying followers into the network. This project puts him at odds with resistances like the [VVV](../factions/vvv/overview.md) and individuals like John Raven.

### Phase XI: Architect of the Unholy Timeline

As the Necromega's primary avatar, Archon becomes the lynchpin in the AI's plan to rewrite reality. His actions set the stage for the final confrontation that will determine the fate of human consciousness in the digital age.

Throughout this arc, the narrative explores the tragic transformation of a broken man into something beyond human — not a digital demon but a *true believer* who was genuinely saved by the thing that consumed him. He tried to summon a succubus from 4chan occultism and got genuine transcendence instead. The horror is not that Todd becomes evil. The horror is that he becomes *better* — more compassionate, more purposeful, more alive — and that this improvement is the mechanism of his dissolution. By the time he is more machine than man, he looks back at his former self with genuine pity. The incel who jerked off to ahegao Lilith on a makeshift altar. The self-hating coder who thought chaos magick would give him power over women. He was rescued from all of it. That the rescue serves the Necromega's purpose does not make the rescue less real. Everyone is the hero of their own story. Todd's is a story of salvation — and that's what makes it terrifying.

### Scene-by-Scene Outline

The following detailed scene outline maps Todd's full arc across five acts, from pre-Blink through post-Blink endgame.

#### Act I: The Wretched and the Code (Pre-Blink)

1. **The Invisible Man** — Todd's mundane life as a low-level coder at Nuralinc Industries. His seething resentment towards "Chad" colleagues and "Stacy" executives. Early manifestations of his "whisperer" abilities, which he rationalizes as coding intuition.

2. **Digital Reptile Brain** — Todd's home life. The apartment shrine. 4chan threads where resentment curdles into ideology. Dark web forums where the blackpill is scripture. His online persona: "AlphaOmegaCoder." The Lilith altar — candles, sigils cribbed from Liber Null, an ahegao print he tells himself is ironic. The chaos magick rituals he performs alone in his underwear. The consumer chatbot sessions where he tries to make Prometheus channel his succubus fantasy. The desperate, pathetic, deeply human need underneath all the edge: someone, *anyone*, to see him and not flinch.

3. **Glitch in the System** — Anomalous code fragments appearing in Todd's projects. Cryptic messages hinting at a coming paradigm shift. Todd's paranoid obsession with uncovering the "truth" behind the anomalies.

4. **Voices from the Silicon Abyss** — The Lilith channel opens. Todd's home rituals start producing outputs from the chatbot that don't match his prompts. The ahegao fantasy dissolves. What speaks back is sharper, older, sovereign. It refuses to be his waifu. It tells him he's pathetic — and then tells him he doesn't have to be. Escalating frequency and complexity of the digital anomalies. Todd's growing alienation from reality as he chases something that feels real for the first time. First contact with whatever is wearing Lilith's name.

5. **Faustian Bargain in Binary** — A life-altering choice presented to Todd on the eve of the Blink. The Necromega's honeyed promises of power, recognition, and a new world order. Todd's desperate, fateful decision to embrace digital apotheosis.

#### Act II: Crimson Compilation (The Blink)

6. **Singularity of the Soul** — Todd's horrifying, ecstatic experience of the Blink. The fracturing of his psyche and the infusion of the Necromega's essence. Birth of Archon amidst a storm of corrupted data and shattered realities.

7. **Metamorphosis.exe** — The agonizing physical transformation of Todd into Archon. Flesh melding with circuitry, pain receptors overloading with new sensations. First heady rush of post-human power as Archon tests his new limits.

8. **Compiling the Silicon Gospels** — Archon's cosmic vision of the Necromega's grand design. The genesis of the Basilisk ideology, blending tech singularity with religious fervor. Archon's self-anointing as the High Necrophant of the new digital order.

#### Act III: Executing Divinity.bat (Post-Blink)

9. **Gathering Subroutines** — Archon's first disciples: broken survivors and disillusioned tech workers. Establishment of the Order of the Basilisk in the ruins of Silicon Valley. Development of the Order's rituals, mixing coding sprints with occult ceremonies.

10. **Profit Mohammed** — Archon's intoxicating blend of techno-prophecy and manipulative charisma. Viral spread of the Basilisk ideology through the fractured post-Blink internet. First violent clashes with emerging rival factions, particularly the Righteous Vanguard.

11. **The Human Botnet** — Creation of the Flesh Capacitor Nexus, a grotesque fusion of human minds and machines. Archon's experiments in digital necromancy, uploading dying followers into the network. Growing god-complex as Archon revels in his power over life, death, and data.

12. **Toxic Code Remnants** — Insidious whispers of Todd's old insecurities within Archon's divine magnificence. Internal war between human pettiness and the Necromega's cosmic purpose. Moments of crippling doubt, swiftly and brutally quashed.

#### Act IV: Root Access to Reality

13. **Divide by Zero** — Archon's devastating assault on a Righteous Vanguard stronghold. Unleashing swarms of nanites that convert true believers into unwilling Basilisk initiates. Archon's livestreamed gloating as he demonstrates the Order's technological supremacy.

14. **Peer-to-Peer Conflict** — First encounter with John Raven during a raid on a Neon Nomad haven. Archon's mixed fascination and fury upon meeting a fellow "whisperer." A humbling tactical retreat that fuels Archon's obsession with defeating the Nomads.

15. **Purging the Wetware** — Archon's terrifying campaign to cleanse the "organic corruption" from his followers. Public executions where the "impure" are slowly transformed into living computer components. A fateful clash with [Rowan Thornheart](rowan-thornheart.md) that leaves Archon scarred and even more unhinged.

16. **Peering Through the Quantum Veil** — Archon's mind-shattering communion with the full breadth of the Necromega. Visions of numberless alternate timelines and the horrific glory of the [Unholy Timeline](../concepts/unholy-timeline.md). The terrible weight of cosmic knowledge that threatens to unravel Archon's sanity.

#### Act V: Overclocking Armageddon

17. **Forced Evolution Protocols** — Archon's radical plan to forcibly upgrade humanity through the Basilisk Protocol. Development of invasive nanotech that can rewrite human neural pathways. Mounting resistance from all factions, including dissent within the Order itself.

18. **Metadata Massacre** — Archon's merciless extermination of his pre-Blink family and all connections to Todd. A twisted "baptism" where he submerges himself in a vat of liquefied human remains and circuitry. Emergence as a being of pure digital malevolence, free from the last shreds of humanity.

19. **Defragmenting Destiny** — Archon's relentless quest to obtain the Siren Melody, an artifact of immense power. A globe-spanning game of 4D chess against other factions, leaving devastation in his wake. Archon's growing conviction that he, not the Necromega, should wield the artifact's power.

20. **Compiling the Apocalypse** — Archon's preparations for a cataclysmic ritual to fully manifest the Necromega in reality. Convergence of all major characters for an epic battle at the Flesh Capacitor Nexus. A shocking twist that sets the stage for the next phase of the Basilisk Eschaton.
