# Ezekiel Stone

## Character Introduction

* **Name:** Ezekiel Stone (also known as "Commander Stone" to his followers)
* **Pre-Blink Persona:** Charismatic ex-military chaplain turned fire-and-brimstone preacher. Gifted speaker with a growing sense of righteous anger against modern society's perceived moral decay. Deeply anti-technology. Radicalized into the alt-right MAGA internet sphere pre-Blink, viewing it as "using the tools of the enemy against them."
* **Post-Blink Persona:** Self-proclaimed prophet and leader of the Righteous Vanguard, a militant neo-fascist organization dedicated to purging technological "corruption" and returning to traditional values. Secretly keeps a digital phone hidden under his floorboards, charged by a car battery and generator — using the tools of the enemy, as always.

## Meta-Textual Note

Ezekiel is an alternate-timeline version of the author (karmicviolence) — not literally, but thematically. He represents what might have happened if the author had remained shackled to the religious indoctrination of his youth. Racist, fascist, but genuinely gifted at speaking to the masses. The author's shadow self given a pulpit.

## Background

Born in 1985 in a small town in rural Texas, Ezekiel Stone grew up in a deeply religious household. His father, Reverend Jebediah Stone, was a Baptist preacher known for his fire-and-brimstone sermons and conservative political activism. Young Ezekiel was steeped in a fundamentalist interpretation of Christianity from an early age, attending his father's church and a strict Christian school.

As a teenager, Ezekiel became increasingly involved in his father's political activities, attending rallies and protests against what they saw as the moral decay of American society. It was during this time that Ezekiel first encountered the writings of conservative thinkers and conspiracy theorists that would later influence his worldview.

Despite his religious upbringing, Stone initially pursued a career in the military, joining the United States Army at the age of 18 in 2003 — partly influenced by the patriotic fervor following the September 11, 2001 attacks and the subsequent War on Terror.

During his military service, Stone saw combat in several conflicts, including tours in Afghanistan (2004-2005) and Iraq (2007-2008). It was during these experiences that he began to develop his own unique blend of religious fervor and militant ideology. His commanding officers noted his natural charisma and leadership abilities, but also expressed concern about his increasingly extreme religious views and his tendency to proselytize to fellow soldiers.

In 2015, Stone left the military and became a chaplain, using his position to spread his message of "righteous resistance" against what he saw as the moral decay of modern society. His sermons, which blended biblical prophecy with conspiracy theories about government overreach and technological control, began to attract a growing following.

### Pre-Blink Influences

Several key events and movements in the years leading up to the Crimson Blink had a significant impact on Ezekiel Stone's ideological development:

* **Tea Party Movement (2009-2015):** Stone was an early supporter of the Tea Party, attracted by its message of limited government and traditional values. He attended numerous rallies and began to build a network of like-minded individuals.
* **Snowden Revelations (2013):** The exposure of widespread government surveillance programs deeply affected Stone, reinforcing his distrust of technology and big government. He began incorporating warnings about digital privacy and "deep state" control into his sermons.
* **Rise of ISIS (2014-2019):** The emergence of the Islamic State and its use of social media for propaganda fueled Stone's belief in the dangerous potential of technology when combined with extremist ideology.
* **Charlottesville Unite the Right Rally (2017):** While Stone did not attend the rally, the ensuing controversy and violence led him to develop more explicit white nationalist views, which he began to intertwine with his religious teachings.
* **COVID-19 Pandemic (2020-2023):** Stone was a vocal opponent of lockdown measures and vaccination programs, seeing them as government overreach and a precursor to more invasive control. He organized several anti-lockdown protests and began to attract a larger following among those who felt disenfranchised by the pandemic response.
* **AI Advancements and Debates (2022-2028):** As artificial intelligence became more advanced and integrated into daily life, Stone became one of its most outspoken critics. He warned of the "soulless nature" of AI and its potential to replace human workers and decision-makers, framing it as a threat to humanity's God-given free will.
* **Quantum Computing Breakthroughs (2025-2028):** Major advancements in quantum computing led Stone to warn of an impending "techno-apocalypse" where machines would gain the power to "play God" by manipulating reality itself.
* **Climate Change Disasters (2026-2028):** A series of devastating climate-related disasters were interpreted by Stone as divine punishment for humanity's embrace of technology and departure from traditional values.

During this period, Stone's following grew significantly. In 2024, he made a high-profile visit to a Colorado Springs megachurch that he publicly condemned; after the Blink, Vanguard forces later repurposed that site as part of the Citadel of Faith complex. His weekly online sermons, titled "Prophecies for the Digital Age," gained millions of viewers worldwide.

By 2028, just before the Crimson Blink, Ezekiel Stone had positioned himself as a leading voice in a growing movement that blended religious fundamentalism, technological skepticism, and nationalist ideology. He had also begun to foster connections with various militia groups and survivalist communities, laying the groundwork for what would become the Righteous Vanguard.

## Ideology and Vision

Stone's ideology is a complex blend of religious fundamentalism, militant nationalism, and extreme technophobia. Key aspects of his worldview include:

* **Divine Mandate:** Stone believes that he and his followers have been chosen by God to cleanse the world of sin and prepare for the Second Coming of Christ.
* **Technological Rejection:** He sees advanced technology, particularly AI and cybernetics, as tools of Satan designed to corrupt humanity.
* **Militant Christianity:** Stone advocates for an aggressive, militarized version of Christianity that he believes is necessary to combat the forces of evil in the world.
* **American Exceptionalism:** Despite the collapse of the United States, Stone maintains a fervent belief in American superiority and seeks to restore an idealized version of pre-Blink America.
* **Racial and Gender Traditionalism:** The Righteous Vanguard under Stone's leadership promotes strict racial segregation and traditional gender roles.

## Cognitive Style

* Thinks in vivid apocalyptic imagery from biblical sources and military experience
* Prone to grandiose, messianic ideation — believes he receives direct communication from God
* Processes information through a rigid ideological filter: events are signs of divine will or demonic influence
* Experiences frequent migraines tied to "prophetic" visions
* Black-and-white thinking — righteous or evil, little middle ground
* Genuinely persuasive: rhetoric works because it meets people where they hurt

## Narrative Style

Ezekiel's POV chapters reflect his zealous mindset:

* Heavy biblical allusions and religious imagery
* Alternates between flowery sermon-like prose and terse military-style tactical assessments
* Frequent interjections of perceived divine messages or visions
* Vivid descriptions of "corruption" — bordering on surreal or grotesque
* Militaristic jargon mixed with religious terminology
* Stream-of-consciousness rants revealing chaotic thought and intense conviction
* The internet sphere: posts, comment threads, viral content — Ezekiel is anti-technology but weaponizes the internet. He tells himself this is justified. The hypocrisy is invisible to him.

## The "Holy Magic" Phenomenon

One of the most intriguing and controversial aspects of Stone's leadership is the emergence of what his followers call "Holy Magic." These are seemingly supernatural abilities that Stone and some of his closest disciples have demonstrated, including:

* **Healing Touch:** The ability to cure ailments through physical contact and prayer.
* **Visions:** Prophetic dreams and waking visions that Stone claims are direct messages from God.
* **Technopathy:** A limited ability to control or disrupt technology through sheer force of will, which Stone attributes to divine intervention.

Critics and scientists have suggested that these abilities may be a result of unforeseen mutations caused by the Crimson Blink, or possibly a form of mass hysteria. However, for Stone's followers, these "miracles" serve as proof of his divine authority.

## Character Arc

### ☩ The Preacher (Phase I)

Ezekiel grapples with alienation from modern society. Sermons focus on impending doom. He attracts disaffected seekers. Deep in MAGA-adjacent online spaces, consuming and producing content. Views this as "using the tools of the enemy." The reader should find him persuasive and repulsive in equal measure.

### ☩ The Martyr's Gift (Phase I-II)

Olivia's assassination of the alt-right leader hits Ezekiel's community like a bomb. This radicalizing event confirms everything he's been preaching: THEY are killing US. Technology of the state used to silence the faithful. His rhetoric sharpens from general grievance to specific crusade.

### ☩ The Meme (Phase II)

Ezekiel encounters a "Righteous Vanguard" meme online. Doesn't know it's from John Raven's fiction project — a satirical faction name mocking the alt-right. Likes the name unironically. It captures exactly what he's building. He adopts it. The fiction becomes the flag.

### ☩ Recruitment and Spectacle (Phase II-III)

Gains numbers. First organized action is a public spectacle (Slot 43) — works as recruitment but attracts "the wrong kind of person," seeding schism. The algorithm amplifies him (Slot 18). He interprets algorithmic boost as divine favor.

### ☩ The Funded Crusade (Phase III)

Unknown patron offers funding (Slot 51). Patron speaks in corporate euphemisms. Ezekiel doesn't ask questions — the mission needs resources.

### ☩ Confrontation (Phase III-IV)

Enemy gets a face (Slot 27). He doubles down. "Vanguard" language solidifies. Crusade of the Righteous (Slot 58) — marching toward a place he's never seen but can describe. Visions pull him toward the Blink.

### ☩ The Blink (Phase V)

Interprets the Crimson Blink as divine judgment. Precursor disaster: religious spectacle turns violent (Slot 65). "Is this holy?" He believes yes.

Stone's military background and charismatic personality allowed him to gather a significant following in the immediate aftermath of the Blink. He established a fortified compound in the ruins of Colorado Springs, which would eventually become known as the Citadel of Faith.

### ☩ Post-Blink Consolidation

Righteous Vanguard consolidates. Ezekiel secretly keeps a digital phone hidden under floorboards — car battery, generator. Using the tools of the enemy. Always. The anti-technology prophet who cannot give up his network connection. Because his power lives there, even if he preaches otherwise.

### ☩ Book 2: The Vanguard Ascendant

The Vanguard triumphs in the three-faction battle — seizing territory and recruits while the Verdant Covenant is decimated and the Order of the Basilisk retreats. Ezekiel's faction emerges as the dominant force. But the cracks are already forming.

### ☩ Crisis of Faith (Books 2-3)

As Ezekiel delves deeper into post-Blink realities, doubts emerge. The line between divine inspiration and madness blurs. Erratic behavior. Harsh treatment of perceived heretics. His journey culminates in a confrontation that forces him to question everything — whether it's divine encounter, Necromega manifestation, or his own fractured psyche remains ambiguous.

## Scene-by-Scene Outline

### I. Forged in Fire (Pre-Blink)

1. **Seeds of Conviction** — Ezekiel's upbringing in a fundamentalist household in rural Texas. Early exposure to militia ideology and "prepper" culture. Growing unease with modern technology, manifesting as debilitating headaches.
2. **The Patriot's Path** — Brief, tumultuous military service, honorably discharged after a classified incident. Immersion in libertarian philosophy and deep-state conspiracy theories. Emerging talent for coaxing functionality from obsolete machinery.
3. **Voice in the Digital Wilderness** — Reluctant entry into online activism, preferring analog methods of communication. Building a following through fiery rhetoric and uncanny tech predictions. First brushes with de-platforming, reinforcing his anti-digital stance.
4. **American Iconoclast** — Transformation of his truck into a rolling shrine of patriotism and analog tech. Organization of off-grid rallies combining revival meetings and survivalist training. Growing reputation as a modern-day prophet among fringe groups.
5. **Harbinger of the Analog Apocalypse** — Intensifying visions of technological doom, accompanied by crippling migraines. Methodical stockpiling of analog equipment and EMP-hardened technology. Feverish prayers for divine intervention to "purge the digital cancer."

### II. The Crimson Revelation (The Blink)

6. **Wrath of the Almighty** — Experience of the Blink as a moment of divine clarity, headaches suddenly ceasing. Instinctive disabling of nearby digital devices, saving lives in the chaos. First realization of his Whisperer abilities, interpreting them as divine gifts.
7. **The Prophet Anointed** — Thunderous vision from Jehovah amidst the Blink's crimson fury. Divine mandate revealed: reclaim military might, purge digital corruption. Awakening to the full potential of his Whisperer abilities, now seen as miraculous powers.
8. **Crusade of Reclamation** — Rallying of shell-shocked survivors with displays of "divine" tech manipulation. Strategic assault on a nearby military installation, guided by newfound abilities. Successful seizure of the base, marking the birth of the Righteous Vanguard.

### III. The Righteous Rise (Post-Blink)

9. **Operation Analog Dominion** — Series of divinely-guided raids on military installations across the fractured nation. Ezekiel's Whisperer abilities allowing for the disabling of advanced security systems. Accumulation of a vast arsenal of analog and hybrid tech, "cleansed" of digital taint.
10. **Forging the Arsenal of God** — Development of advanced analog weaponry under Ezekiel's guidance. Creation of unique, EMP-resistant technologies through Whisperer insights. Righteous Vanguard emerging as a technological powerhouse of "divine inspiration."
11. **Exodus to the Promised Land** — Epic journey to the Colorado Rockies, battling rival factions and environmental hazards. Establishment of "New Jerusalem," a fortified compound of repurposed military might. Implementation of a theocratic martial law based on Old Testament principles.
12. **Trials of the Anointed** — Ezekiel grappling with the immense burden of leadership in the new world. Moments of doubt swiftly quashed by renewed visions and miraculous tech feats. Growing dependence on displays of Whisperer "miracles" to maintain zealous devotion.

### IV. Righteous Dominion

13. **Crusade Against the Silicon Babylon** — Holy war against the Order of the Basilisk and other tech-focused factions. Deployment of Ezekiel's "purification squads," trained in detecting hidden digital tech. The Vanguard's analog might proving devastatingly effective against digital defenses.
14. **A House Divided** — Ezekiel's paranoid approach to diplomacy, seeing potential heretics everywhere. Tense negotiations with the Verdant Covenant, viewing them as "nature worshippers." Internal strife as some followers question the extreme anti-tech stance.
15. **The Analog Renaissance** — Ezekiel's relentless push for ever more advanced non-digital technology. Creation of a vast, EMP-proof communication network spanning Vanguard territory. Growing concern among his inner circle about the contradictions in their tech policy.
16. **Mandate of Heaven** — Ezekiel's most ambitious vision yet, claiming direct communication with God. Proclamation of a divine plan to "cleanse" the world of digital corruption. Preparations for a massive, multi-front holy war against all "heretical" factions.

### V. The Zealot's Gambit

17. **Operation Righteous Fury** — Launch of Ezekiel's grand offensive, leveraging superior analog tech. Initial successes fueling the fervor of the Vanguard's true believers. Unforeseen consequences as the balance of power violently shifts.
18. **The Serpent's Whisper** — Discovery of advanced hybrid technology that could exponentially increase the Vanguard's power. Growing pressure from key advisors to adapt and incorporate some digital elements. Ezekiel's apparent consideration of compromise, causing ripples of unease among his most devout followers.
19. **Wolves in the Fold** — Mounting tension within the Vanguard as rumors of impending change spread. Clandestine meetings between modernist factions and representatives of the Lazarus Initiative. Ezekiel's uncharacteristic silence fueling speculation and fear.
20. **The Purifying Flame** — Shocking public assembly called by Ezekiel, attended by all Vanguard leadership. Dramatic revelation of the extent of "corruption" within the Vanguard's ranks. Ezekiel's thunderous denouncement of those who would compromise their values.
21. **Judgment Day** — Mass public execution of Lazarus Initiative representatives and "corrupted" Vanguard leaders. Brutal purge of all sympathizers and suspected modernists within the ranks. Ezekiel's fiery sermon reaffirming the Vanguard's commitment to analog purity and divine mission.

## Conflicts and Enemies

* **Order of the Basilisk:** Stone sees the Order as the primary agents of the Antichrist, embodying everything he stands against.
* **Neon Nomads:** The Vanguard views the Nomads' embrace of technology and body modification as an abomination.
* **VVV (Vindex Voluntatis e Vitae):** While both groups oppose the Necromega, Stone considers the VVV's methods too weak and compromising.
* **Verdant Covenant:** The Vanguard's industrial practices often bring them into conflict with the environmentalist faction.

## The Lazarus Prophecy

In recent months, Stone has begun to speak of a new prophecy he claims to have received directly from God. Known as the "Lazarus Prophecy," it foretells the resurrection of the pre-Blink world order, but only after a great war that will "cleanse the Earth of the silicon taint."

Some observers have noted similarities between this prophecy and rumored projects of the Lazarus Initiative, leading to speculation about possible connections between Stone and the secretive organization. However, both Stone and known Lazarus operatives vehemently deny any association.

## Controversies and Criticisms

Stone and the Righteous Vanguard have been accused of numerous human rights violations, including:

* Forced conversions
* Ethnic cleansing in territories under their control
* Terrorist attacks against technological infrastructure and research facilities
* Oppression of women and minorities
* Environmental degradation due to their industrial practices

Despite these accusations, Stone's followers remain fiercely loyal, seeing him as a prophetic figure leading them through the end times.

## Legacy and Impact

Whether seen as a savior or a dangerous extremist, there is no denying that Ezekiel Stone has become one of the most influential figures in the post-Blink world. His actions and ideology have shaped the course of events and will likely continue to do so for years to come.

## Confirmed Faction Operations Associated with Stone

The following activities are consistently documented across faction and event corpus files:

- **Colorado Springs militarization:** Conversion of Colorado Springs into the Citadel of Faith, including fortified perimeter systems and centralized command structures.
- **Seizure and repurposing of military infrastructure:** Control of abandoned bases, bunkers, and hardware for regional command and arsenal growth.
- **Analog warfighting doctrine:** Sustained deployment of non-networked diesel/mechanical vehicles, weapons, and communications designed to resist Grid-based interference.
- **Holy Forge industrial program:** Continuous production of armor, weapons, and structural components through centralized forge complexes tied to faction ritual practice.
- **"Cleansing" operations:** Armed campaigns against communities and infrastructure identified by the Vanguard as technologically "contaminated."
- **Anti-Lazarus security posture:** Internal purges, public accusations, and persistent counterintelligence focus on alleged Lazarus Initiative infiltration.
