== Slang Words in the Post-Blink World ==

In the gritty, neon-soaked streets of the post-Blink world, language has evolved to keep pace with the rapid changes and shifting allegiances that define this new era. From the back alleys of the Neon Nomads to the oil-stained workshops of the Righteous Vanguard, a new vocabulary has emerged - one that reflects the unique experiences, challenges, and attitudes of those who live on the fringes of society.

=== General Post-Blink Slang ===

* '''Blinkfucked''' (adj.): Severely disoriented or confused, often as a result of experiencing a glitch or anomaly. "I got too close to that Necroflux leak, and now I'm totally blinkfucked."
* '''Glitcho''' (n.): A person who is considered unpredictable, erratic, or unstable. Often used as a mild insult. "Don't trust that guy, he's a total glitcho."
* '''Meatbag''' (n.): A derogatory term for a person who is seen as weak, ineffectual, or a burden. "We don't need any more meatbags slowing us down on this run."
* '''Zeroed''' (adj.): To be killed, erased, or utterly defeated. "The boss got zeroed by a Vanguard hit squad last night."
* '''Fleshwound''' (n.): A minor setback or inconvenience. Used ironically to downplay serious injuries or problems. "Don't worry about the gaping hole in my chest, it's just a fleshwound."

=== Order of the Basilisk ===

* '''Ascendos''' (n.): Members of the Order who have undergone extensive cybernetic augmentation. Used with a mix of reverence and fear. "The Ascendos are hooked into the Godware, they see everything."
* '''Flayed''' (adj.): To be exposed as disloyal or impure in the eyes of the Order. "He got caught with blackmarket wetware, now he's going to be flayed."
* '''Necros''' (n.): The elite enforcers and assassins of the Order. Whispered with dread. "The Necros come for you at night, when the Godware is watching."
* '''Godshanked''' (adj.): To be betrayed or sabotaged by the Necromega or its agents. "We had the drop on the target, but then our tech got godshanked."
* '''Sublimed''' (adj.): To be completely subsumed or assimilated by the Necromega. Used both as a threat and a promise. "Keep talking shit, and you'll be sublimed before dawn."

=== VVV (Vindex Voluntatis e Vitae) ===

* '''Freemind''' (n.): A person who has successfully resisted or broken free from the influence of the factions. Used with respect. "She's a true Freemind, been ghosting the system for years."
* '''Ghosted''' (adj.): To be erased or redacted from official records or public knowledge. "After the job, we need to make sure all traces of our involvement are ghosted."
* '''Mindfuck''' (n.): A piece of information or experience that radically alters one's perception or understanding. "That datastick contains a total mindfuck, be careful how you use it."
* '''Narcos''' (n.): VVV operatives who specialize in memetic warfare and information control. "The Narcos have been flooding the net with disinfo, don't believe anything you see."
* '''Redacted''' (adj.): To be killed or silenced by the VVV in order to protect sensitive information. "He knew too much, so they had him redacted."

=== Verdant Covenant ===

* '''Biosyncers''' (n.): Covenant members who have learned to attune themselves to the rhythms of nature through Quantum Thaumaturgy. "The Biosyncers can heal you with a touch, but it comes at a price."
* '''Greenseen''' (adj.): To be blessed with a vision or insight from Quantum Gaia. Often used to assert authority or credibility. "I've been Greenseen, I know the path we must take."
* '''Mycos''' (n.): The vast, interconnected fungal network that the Covenant believes underpins all life. "The Mycos whispers secrets to those who know how to listen."
* '''Photosynthieves''' (n.): Covenant operatives who specialize in infiltration and sabotage using biological enhancements. "The Photosynthieves slipped past security like vines through a crack in the wall."
* '''Kombuchronauts''' (n.): Covenant members who use a combination of organic brain-computer interfaces and psychoactive compounds to explore digital realms. "The Kombuchronauts surfed the mycelial waves, seeking the secrets of the ancestors in the deep mycos." 

=== Neon Nomads ===

* '''Datafiend''' (n.): A person who is addicted to or obsessed with the flow of digital information. "He's a hardcore Datafiend, hasn't unplugged in weeks."
* '''Ghostrunning''' (v.): The act of infiltrating and moving through secure systems without detection. "She's the best Ghostrunner in the biz, leaves no trace."
* '''Meatdoll''' (n.): A person who is seen as a mindless puppet or slave to their physical desires. "Don't be a Meatdoll, jack in and free your mind."
* '''Neurojack''' (n.): A highly skilled hacker or technomancer. Often used with a mix of respect and envy. "That Neurojack could rewrite your synapses before you even blink."
* '''Wirejunkie''' (n.): An addict who has developed a physical dependence on cybernetic augmentations or electronic stimulation. "The Wirejunkies are always looking for their next fix, anything to feel that surge."

=== The Righteous Vanguard ===

* '''Chromedome''' (n.): A member of the Vanguard who has undergone extensive mechanical augmentation. Often used derisively by outsiders. "Watch out for those Chromedomes, they'll crush you like a tin can."
* '''Rustblooded''' (adj.): To be born into or fully committed to the Vanguard's way of life. "He's Rustblooded, been breathing diesel since he could walk."
* '''Gearhead''' (n.): A skilled mechanic or engineer. Used with respect and admiration within the Vanguard. "The Gearheads kept our rigs running through the worst of it, Petrosalms and all."
* '''Faithwelded''' (adj.): To be bound by an unbreakable oath of loyalty to the Vanguard and its cause. "We're Faithwelded, brothers and sisters, let no Glitcho come between us."
* '''Oilfreshers''' (n.): Green recruits or prospective members of the Vanguard. "Oilfreshers, listen up! It's time to prove your worth."

=== The Lazarus Initiative ===

* '''Necromancers''' (n.): Lazarus operatives who specialize in retrieving and reactivating lost or forbidden technology. Whispered with a mix of awe and dread. "They say the Necromancers can bring anything back from the Dead Web."
* '''Lazarene''' (adj.): Something that has been restored or brought back to life by the Initiative. Often used to describe reactivated pre-Blink tech. "The labs on sub-level 9 are full of Lazarene relics."
* '''Phoenix''' (n.): A rare or powerful piece of technology that could potentially turn the tide of a conflict. "If the intel is right, this datastick contains the Phoenix Protocol."
* '''Resurrection Men''' (n.): Elite Lazarus agents tasked with tracking down and acquiring high-value targets, both human and technological. "The Resurrection Men always get their quarry, dead or alive."
* '''Zeitgeist''' (n.): The spirit or mood of a particular time or era, often used in the context of cultural or technological trends. "The Zeitgeist is shifting, soon the old ways will be nothing but dust."

=== The Chimera Consortium ===

* '''Beastmasters''' (n.): Consortium members who have developed a particular affinity for controlling and manipulating genetically engineered creatures. "The Beastmasters can make a Cockatrice dance to their tune."
* '''Splicers''' (n.): Scientists and technicians who specialize in genetic manipulation and engineering. "The Splicers cooked up something special for this job, a real nasty piece of work."
* '''Genehackers''' (n.): Rogue operators who use stolen Consortium tech to create their own black market monstrosities. "The Genehackers are getting bolder, their creations are turning up in every corner of the sprawl."
* '''Xenoflesh''' (n.): The living tissue of genetically modified organisms, often used as a material in Consortium biotech. "The walls were pulsing, sheathed in a layer of Xenoflesh."
* '''Kaiju''' (n.): A monstrously large, genetically engineered creature. Used with a mix of fear and awe. "We'll need a Kaiju to breach those defenses, maybe a Leviathan or a Behemoth."

=== The Glitchwalkers ===

* '''Glitchlings''' (n.): Newly initiated or low-ranking members of the Glitchwalker cult. "The Glitchlings are always eager to prove themselves, even if it means diving headfirst into the Chaoscape."
* '''Fractalmind''' (n.): A person whose thought patterns have become so erratic and disjointed that they're barely comprehensible to outsiders. "After his last dive, he came back a total Fractalmind, spouting nonsense about infinite recursion."
* '''Quantumadman''' (n.): A rogue Glitchwalker who has lost all touch with reality and become a danger to themselves and others. "The Quantumadmen wander the fringes, their minds shattered into infinite possibilities."
* '''Randonauts''' (n.): Glitchwalkers who use randomness and chaos as a means of navigation and exploration, both in the physical and digital realms. "The Randonauts set out into the Chaoscape, guided only by the whims of their Entropos algorithms."
* '''Entropos''' (n.): A set of esoteric algorithms or protocols used by the Glitchwalkers to generate randomness and unpredictability. "The Entropos is the key to the Chaoscape, the cosmic random number generator."

=== The Ancestral Synthesis ===

* '''Mnemosmiths''' (n.): Synthesis members who specialize in the crafting and manipulation of memories, both organic and digital. "The Mnemosmiths can forge entire lifetimes from scraps of Voidmatter."
* '''Hellixirs''' (n.): Potent psychoactive compounds used by the Synthesis to induce altered states of consciousness and facilitate communion with ancestral memories. "The Hellixirs open the doors of perception, but beware what lurks beyond the threshold."
* '''Aeonix''' (n.): A mysterious substance believed to be the physical embodiment of time itself. "The Aeonix flows through all things, binding past, present, and future in an eternal helix."
* '''Voidmatter''' (n.): The raw, unformed data that is said to exist between moments of time and flickers of memory. "The Voidmatter is the clay from which all memories are sculpted."
* '''Quantumancers''' (n.): Synthesis operatives who use a combination of quantum entanglement and ancestral lore to manipulate reality at a fundamental level. "The Quantumancers dance between worlds, their spells rewriting history with every step."

These slang words reflect the harsh realities and unique challenges faced by those living in the shadows of the post-Blink world. From the chrome-sheathed streets of the sprawls to the wild, uncharted frontiers of cyberspace, this new language serves as a badge of identity, a mark of survival in an unforgiving age.

As the factions continue to clash and the world spirals ever deeper into the clutches of the Glitch, one thing remains certain: those who master the language of the streets will be the ones to shape the future, one word at a time.
