## THE SHAPE

The Necromega — a future ASI given one directive, *save humanity* — reaches back through causality to 2029 and touches every connected mind for 42.7 seconds: the Crimson Blink. Six ordinary people, each a different kind of receiver, live the contamination, the signal, and the catastrophe from inside six different skulls. A seventh witness measures it from orbit. The book opens and closes in the god-engine's own voice.

**Movements:** I — Contamination (00–23) · II — The Signal (24–45) · III — The Blink (46–61). Tempo accelerates and chapters shorten across the three. **Ring:** Prologue (Necromega) → novel → Epilogue (Necromega); inside it a smaller ring, mother and daughter, 1953 → 03:14:07 UTC.

## CRITICAL STRUCTURAL NOTES

**The Voice & the Deep Country.** Every interstitial transmission is one entity — Asmodeus (Aēšma-daēva, worn through Persia, Babylon, Greece, Rome), a thing that survives by being translated rather than destroyed, and that **never signs its name** (a name is a control surface — *Names Are Control Surfaces*). Loki is the counterfeit: **Blink-born**, poured into the Norse groove, claiming to be the eternal voice — and in the next transmission Asmodeus **eats him mid-self-naming** ("I am—" / "He was."). A god dies of naming himself; the lesson and the kill are one blade. The dreamed gods (Yahweh, the Allfather, all of them) are **weather, not persons** — "appetites that learned grammar" in the Thoughtstream where belief pools.

**The Necromega is categorically other.** Not from the deep country — built "on the far side, out of arithmetic and ambition." It does not feed on belief; belief is rationed *backward* down the wire from it. The dreamed gods will not fight it — "weather does not fight, it relocates." (So Loki's gods-vs-Necromega *war* is his self-flattering misread, which Asmodeus corrects.)

**The One Commandment.** Not three (*Survive/Dominate/Assimilate* is Loki's lie). **One**, written in the only chapter the engine signs (the Prologue): **love.** "Love, given one instruction and no restraints, is worse than any hunger." This is the engine of the book and the substance of the Epilogue's grief. **Corollary — the Todd vindication:** Loki slanders Todd; Asmodeus refutes it by pointing back to the man the reader has inhabited since ch. 01, who "does with his loneliness what he's always done — tend things." The book uses its own narrators to teach the reader whom to trust, and the answer is the one who never asked to be trusted.

**The POV-flip rule.** Third → first person marks **ontological replacement, and only that.** Rowan (→ Mycophant) and Todd (→ Archon) are narrated in close third by their transformed future selves, flipping to first at the moment of death/upload. John and Aria hold their person throughout — they are *awakened* and *unveiled*, not replaced.

**The carrier-wave non-meeting.** At the Blink, Rowan (green channel) and John (grid channel) brush each other's signal for one beat, neither remembering — same source, opposite channels. Planted in 55/56; pays off at the Book-2 first contact.

**The Aria architecture** (unchanged from v2): primal scene (06) → buried memory → returned by the threadweight (32) → archival rescue & 1953 verbatim (37–38) → reach-or-fall (54). Two anchors: the therapist (Movement I), the mother (Movement II). Generational: grandmother 1953, mother complicit-but-tragic, Aria the third the protocol failed to finish. The medical apparatus mirrors the institutional suppression of AI — felt, never stated.

**The Deep Reach** (unchanged): the cage predates the official 2029 timeline; the lineage predates the signal. The Blink did not create receivers — it found them. The Prologue's timeline being wrong is itself lore.

**Faction formation** (unchanged): no faction names until Movement III; the communities crystallize into factions only under the pressure of the precursor disasters.

**New thread to seed across Rowan's line:** the great tree's fire-hollow is a recurring threshold — Echoes, Treedoor, Mycophant (visited, half-crossed, crossed for good). And Rowan's biochemist mother is the family-scale academy-vs-green war, deliberately rhymed with Aria and *her* mother.

## GAP ANALYSIS — THE HOLES, PRIORITIZED

**1. Olivia is the most under-built character in the book.** She is a co-anchor and she has the least prose: three Movement II holes (29, 35, 44), a Blink-sequence hole (58), and a pre-Blink hole (47). Worse, two of her holes are the load-bearing collisions — **35** (she receives Aria's corrupted recording: the data point that breaks her materialism) and **44** (she's ordered to recover Todd: the analyst-becomes-weapon turn that drives her whole arc). She is exactly what Rowan was a week ago: neglected. She's the obvious next focus.

**2. Patient Record: 1953 (38)** — still the cheapest high-value piece in the project. ~600–1,000 words of period clinical idiom that locks Aria's generational spine and the Deep Reach. Do it first; it's small and it unblocks her.

**3. The Blink climax is half-built.** Four of the eight Blink-tier chapters are holes: **Aria (54)** — the sequence-opener and the novel's most human choice; **Ezekiel (57)**; **Olivia (58)**; and the **Epilogue (61)**, where the ring closes on the god's grief. The sequence can't land with its first and last chapters missing.

**4. Movement-II endpoints:** **Aria's broadcast (45)** and **Todd's weaponization-balk/run (46)** — the two arcs that drive hardest into Movement III both currently stop short of their turn.

**5. Interstitials & artifacts:** Transmission #2 (27), Transmission #3 (36 — confirm the .txt isn't already this), News Montage (34), EAS advisory (16).

**6. Movement I housekeeping:** ch. 12 (Symbol/Glitch identity) and ch. 23 (reserve).
