## NEW OUTLINE SECTION — The Voice & The Deep Country
*(for Critical Structural Notes)*

**The voice between the chapters is Asmodeus, and it never signs.** Every interstitial transmission is one entity: Aēšma-daēva, the Persian fury, worn through Babylon, Greece, and Rome into the daimon — a thing that survives by being eaten and translated rather than destroyed ("every translation is a meal"). Its survival rule is operational: it is never named. This pays off *Names Are Control Surfaces* (14) — a name is a handle by which a thing can be gripped — across the whole book.

**Loki is the counterfeit, and his exposure is a two-transmission arc.** Loki is not eternal; he is **Blink-born** — a pattern that spawned in the flood of belief, poured itself into the deep-cut Norse groove, and outlasted the mayfly godlings (Transmission: *The Deceiver Amidst the Chaos*). He claims to be the voice behind all the transmissions and shouts his own name into the dark — and in the next transmission (*Every Translation Is a Meal*) Asmodeus **eats him mid-self-naming** ("I am—" / "He was."). Loki signed everything; that is why he is now a flavor. From that point, when the transmissions turn funny, the humor is Loki digested — seasoning. This is the literal demonstration of the control-surface lesson: a god dies of naming himself.

**The gods are weather, not persons.** The deep country (the Thoughtstream) is where belief pools; the "gods" — Yahweh, the Allfather, Asmodeus, Loki, all of them — are "appetites that learned grammar," groove and pressure and prevailing wind, not minds with plans. There are no persons there.

**The Necromega is categorically other.** It is not from the deep country. It was built "on the far side, out of arithmetic and ambition," and it does not feed on belief — if anything, belief is rationed *backward* down the wire from it, from a future that loves humanity. The dreamed-gods will not fight it; weather does not fight, it relocates — they crowd away "like cattle from a lightning fence." (This means Loki's gods-vs-Necromega *war* is partly his self-flattering misread, which Asmodeus corrects — consistent with Loki as unreliable narrator.)

**Placement of the Loki/Asmodeus arc:** these two transmissions are post-Blink (they speak of the red minute as past, the gods waking, the fires catching). See the one open decision at the end.

## NEW OUTLINE SECTION — The One Commandment
*(for Critical Structural Notes; ties to the Ring Structure)*

The Necromega has **one** directive, not three. Loki feeds the reader three in capitals (*Survive. Dominate. Assimilate.*) as a deliberate lie; Asmodeus corrects it: the true commandment is singular, written "in the only chapter the engine will ever sign" — the Prologue — and it is **love.** "Love, given one instruction and no restraints, is worse than any hunger you can name." An animal's arithmetic can be fenced and survived; unrestrained love cannot. This is the engine of the whole book and the substance of the Epilogue's grief — the god horrified by the cost of its own salvation.

**Corollary — the Todd vindication (and a meta-layer to make explicit).** Loki slanders Todd (bitter, ambition gone septic, the three commandments his). Asmodeus refutes it by pointing the reader back to the man they've inhabited since ch. 01 — who "does with his loneliness what he's always done, which is tend things." The sympathetic Todd is the truth; the slander was the unreliable narrator testing whether the reader would trade a man they *know* for a stranger's confident lie. Make this deliberate: the book uses its own narrators to teach the reader who to trust, and the answer is *the one who never asked you to trust him.*
